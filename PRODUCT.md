# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ServiceOS serves a local cleaning and house-help agency, its customers, its field workers, and its office administrators. Customers arrive with a practical need: book a cleaning visit, request laundry, arrange an Airbnb turnover, or start a house-help placement. Workers need clear job details, route context, status updates, and pay visibility. Admin staff need a calm operating view for bookings, assignment, payments, follow-up, and placement requests.

## Product Purpose

ServiceOS is the operating layer for a service business that sells household and commercial cleaning, laundry, Airbnb turnover, and house-help placement. Success means a customer can understand the offer and book quickly, while the agency can see what needs action today without losing payment, assignment, or quality details.

## Brand Personality

Fresh, orderly, local, and dependable. The public website should feel clean and service-ready without becoming generic template marketing. The product surfaces should feel direct, legible, and operational, with enough warmth for a people-powered service business.

## Anti-references

Avoid bland SaaS dashboards, oversized stat-card grids, heavy dark admin panels, over-rounded template cards, thin gray text, generic blue-only palettes, and marketing copy that claims more than the current app demonstrates. Avoid visual systems that obscure the booking task or make field work feel like a corporate analytics product.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next action obvious on every surface.
2. Use service evidence: schedules, staff states, payment statuses, and customer locations should drive the interface.
3. Keep public pages bright and confident, while keeping operations screens denser and calmer.
4. Treat booking as a guided workflow, not a form dump.
5. Preserve local specificity: Malindi, M-Pesa, WhatsApp, verified workers, and placement services are part of the product.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast, visible focus states, readable form labels, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts for mobile customers and workers. Do not rely on color alone for status.
